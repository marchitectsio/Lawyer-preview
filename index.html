Immediate priorities after an arrest

  • Stay silent. Do not try to talk your way out.
  • Request a lawyer and decline interviews.
  • Ask family to document names, witnesses, and any video sources.
  • Do not contact alleged victims or witnesses.
  • Call our office to plan bail, arraignment, and next steps.

What happens next

A clear three step process

Criminal cases feel chaotic because most people have never seen the system from the inside. This is the core timeline we use to bring order to the moment and make strategic decisions early.

Step 1

The arrest

Stay calm and stay silent. Do not try to explain the situation to police. Ask for a lawyer. Document names, locations, and any witnesses the moment you can do so safely.

Step 2

The arraignment

This is the first court appearance. Bail and protective orders are often decided here. We appear with you, ask for reasonable release conditions, and set the case up for strong defense work.

Step 3

Pre trial hearings

We challenge the evidence. That includes suppression motions, credibility issues, and technical flaws in testing or police procedure. Many cases are won here long before a trial date.
